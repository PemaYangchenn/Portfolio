Flower Catalogue Website

The Flower Catalogue Website is a mobile website designed to serve as a comprehensive digital catalog for florists.

Project Overview

  • The Product: The Flower Catalogue App is a mobile application designed to serve as a comprehensive digital catalog for florists.
  • Duration: March 2024 to May 2024
  • The Problem: Traditional floral catalog systems are cumbersome and difficult to manage, necessitating a modern solution that simplifies ordering, offers diverse floral offerings, and streamlines the process for florists and customers.
  • Goal: The primary goal of the Flower Catalogue App is to provide florists with a user-friendly digital platform to showcase their products.

Role:

  • A UX/UI designer who will conduct market research,
    create wireframes and prototypes, and design an intuitive,
    visually appealing interface for the Flower Catalogue Website project.


    • Responsibilities:

  • Conducting interviews, paper and digital wireframing,
    low and high-fidelity prototyping, conducting usability studies, accounting for accessibility, and iterating on designs.

    • User Research Summary

    User research revealed florists require robust features like real-time inventory management, personalized arrangement options, and efficient customer communication, leading to a more comprehensive and user-friendly Flower Catalogue Website.

    User Persona

    Problem statement: Lhazin is a dedicated and creative florist owner who needs an efficient digital catalog and inventory management system because her current manual processes are outdated and prone to errors, leading to missed sales opportunities and customer dissatisfaction.

    Problem statement: Namgay is a tech-savvy and ambitious boutique florist owner who needs an efficient digital catalog and inventory management system because her current manual processes are inefficient and limit her ability to reach a wider audience and provide a seamless customer experience.

    Paper Wireframe

    Establishing the fundamental layout of a page and emphasizing the intended purpose of each piece were the two main objectives of the wireframe creation process. Prioritizing the structure and function above the beautiful components was my goal. I was able to quickly generate ideas and iterate on my concept while working on the paper wireframe. I could picture my ideas the cheapest and most easily with it. I made many wireframes and organized the site according to its intended use.

    Digital Wireframe

    Digital wireframes focus on the placement of elements such as buttons, navigation bars, images, and text, without delving into aesthetic details like colors or fonts.

    Low-fidelity prototype

    These prototypes are instrumental in validating design concepts, refining user flows, and gathering feedback from stakeholders in the early stages of the design process.

    User Research

    To ensure the Flower Catalogue Website meets the needs of florists like Namgay, we conducted a series of usability studies. These studies aimed to identify any usability issues, gather user feedback, and understand how florists interact with the website. Participants included florists of varying tech-savviness, allowing us to gather a diverse range of insights. The findings from these studies will inform the websites's iterative design and development process.

    Takeaways

    • Impact: The design of the Flower Catalogue App has received positive feedback from peers and study participants, with one stating, "The app's intuitive interface and accessibility features make it incredibly easy to find and order the perfect floral arrangement." This feedback underscores the app's potential to enhance customer satisfaction and streamline the purchasing process for florists.


    • What I Learned: The Flower Catalogue Website project highlighted user-centered design, accessibility, and collaboration, ensuring a user-friendly, effective, and accessible product that aligns with technical feasibility and business objectives.
